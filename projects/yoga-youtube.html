Decoding Yoga Video Trends
on YouTube

Personal Project  ·  Python · NLTK · Tableau

My journey into wellness began during my PhD when a 21-day yoga camp on campus introduced me to the transformative power of yoga. This project combines that personal passion with data science — using NLP and audience behaviour analysis to uncover what actually drives engagement on yoga YouTube channels, and turning those findings into actionable content strategy insights.

PythonNLTKPandas CountVectorizerTableauNLP Time-Series AnalysisData Visualisation

Overview

Using a Kaggle dataset of yoga YouTube video metadata (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0), I analysed video attributes including release dates, duration, views, likes, and comments to understand what makes yoga content perform. The analysis spans three interconnected approaches: time-series engagement trends, correlation analysis between duration and performance metrics, and NLP-driven title keyword analysis.

Methodology

Three Analytical Lenses

01
Feature Engineering — Created a custom "Engagement Ratio" metric combining views, likes, and comments to normalise performance across videos of different ages.
02
Natural Language Processing — Applied tokenization and lemmatization (NLTK) to video titles, then used CountVectorizer to extract the most impactful keywords by engagement ratio.
03
Exploratory Data Analysis — Mapped engagement trends over time, across days of the week, and by video duration to identify structural patterns in the data.

Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Mon & Thu

Peak Release Days

Videos released on Mondays and Thursdays generate significantly higher early engagement.

< 35 min

Sweet Spot Duration

Shorter videos and yoga challenges consistently outperform longer-form content on engagement ratio.

2.5×

Informal Language Boost

Titles using informal, relatable terms ('studio', 'baby', 'welcome') perform over 2.5× better than generic ones.

April

Highest Release Volume

April sees peak content output — likely tied to spring wellness seasonality and new-year momentum.

"Morning yoga videos dominate engagement — and time-specific keywords like '10 min', '30 min', 'morning' are among the highest-performing title elements."

Actionable Insights

→
Leverage challenge formats — Yoga challenges generate disproportionate engagement; creators should prioritise structured series over one-off videos.
→
Optimise titles with time-specific keywords — Phrases like "10 min morning yoga" signal immediate value and perform measurably better.
→
Embrace informal, relatable language — Community-feel language in titles outperforms formal or instructional phrasing by a wide margin.
