The Sweet Truth Detector

Unveiling Hidden Sweeteners in Packaged Foods

Personal Project  ·  OpenCV · SpaCy · Scikit-learn · OCR

Ever wondered how much "hidden sugar" sneaks into your favourite packaged foods? From invert syrup to maltitol, sweeteners often disguise themselves under unfamiliar names. This project builds a machine learning-powered pipeline that detects sweeteners in ingredient lists — whether you provide text directly or an image of the packaging — and visually highlights them.

OpenCVSpaCyScikit-learn TF-IDFRandom ForestTesseract OCR NLPComputer VisionImage Annotation

The Problem

Hidden sugars in packaged food items are often overlooked despite their significant impact on health. Identifying them requires knowing dozens of aliases — chemical names, brand names, and industry terms that most consumers have never heard of. The Sweet Truth Detector makes this identification automatic, accurate, and accessible.

Pipeline Architecture

End-to-End in Four Stages

01

Dataset Creation

Built two labelled datasets: individual ingredients tagged as sweetener (1) or non-sweetener (0), plus a synthetic dataset of 1,000+ ingredient lists with "Sweeteners?" labels for training the classifier.

02

Text Preprocessing & ML Classification

Used SpaCy and regular expressions to clean and normalise ingredient text. Applied TF-IDF vectorisation and trained a Random Forest Classifier to predict sweetener presence.

03

OCR & Image Processing

Preprocessed food label images in OpenCV (grayscale conversion, adaptive thresholding) and extracted text using Tesseract OCR — feeding directly into the sweetener detection model.

04

Annotated Output

The pipeline produces an annotated image with bounding boxes highlighting detected sweeteners, plus a full list of all sweeteners found in the ingredient list.

Why It Matters

Identifying hidden sweeteners empowers consumers to make informed choices — especially people managing diabetes, insulin resistance, or sugar-related health concerns. The Sweet Truth Detector demonstrates that healthcare-focused AI can be both technically robust and genuinely useful for everyday decisions.
